We all have a digital self. It's just scattered across hundreds of accounts, profiles, and platforms — each one knowing a slim sliver of who we are, none of them knowing the whole.

What if instead of one identity per platform, you had **one identity, period** — a graph you owned, that platforms queried with your consent? Your taste, your work, your history, your network — all woven into a single self-graph that belonged to you and traveled with you.

## What I'm trying to build

A system where every interaction adds to your self-graph. Where consent is granular, revocable, and auditable. Where the data dividend flows back to the person who generated it.

This is the long-form thinking that sits behind the Founder Memo. The memo is the *what* and *who's funding*. This essay is the *why it matters*.
